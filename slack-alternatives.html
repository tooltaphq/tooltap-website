The real reasons
Why people leave Slack
Based on analysis of 4,000+ user reviews and community discussions — these are the most common switching triggers in 2026.
Reason #1
Notification overload
Teams report drowning in notifications with important messages buried in endless channel scrolls. The promise of "organised communication" has broken down for many.
Reason #2
Expensive for growing teams
Slack's per-user pricing becomes painful fast. At $7.25/user/month, a 20-person team pays ₹14,000+/month — for a chat tool.
Reason #3
Resource heavy & slow
74% of Capterra reviewers flag performance issues — high memory usage, slow load times, and frequent sluggishness especially on older machines.
Reason #4
Messages get lost
Information scattered across DMs, group DMs, channels, and threads — making it nearly impossible to find anything important from last week.
Reason #5
90-day message limit
Free plan restricts message history to 90 days — forcing teams to pay or lose their entire conversation history.
Established alternatives
6 proven tools people switch to
Ranked by switching frequency — not paid placement.
Microsoft Teams
Enterprise communication bundled with Microsoft 365
Most switched to Free plan
The most common Slack destination — especially for companies already using Microsoft 365. Teams bundles chat, video, file storage, and Office apps in one subscription. It's more corporate-feeling than Slack, but for enterprise teams already paying for Microsoft 365, it's effectively free.
Best for
Companies already on Microsoft 365 who want to consolidate tools and eliminate a separate Slack subscription.
Free plan available · Paid from $6/user/month
Visit Teams
Discord
Voice-first community platform — now used by startups & dev teams
Free
Discord started as a gaming platform but has become the tool of choice for many startups, dev teams, and communities. Voice channels for spontaneous conversations, organized servers, and a completely free model make it compelling — especially for remote-first teams who want a less formal environment.
Best for
Startups, dev teams, and community-driven organizations who want real-time voice collaboration without the enterprise feel of Slack.
Free · Nitro from $9.99/month
Visit Discord
Google Chat
Built into Google Workspace — zero additional cost
Included in Workspace
If your team uses Google Workspace, Google Chat is already included at no extra cost. It integrates natively with Gmail, Google Drive, Meet, and Docs. Less feature-rich than Slack, but for teams living in Google's ecosystem, the integration depth more than compensates.
Best for
Teams already on Google Workspace who want to eliminate Slack costs without switching their entire tool stack.
Included in Google Workspace from $6/user/month
Visit Google Chat
Mattermost
Open-source, self-hosted Slack alternative
Free self-hosted Privacy-first
Mattermost is the open-source Slack — looks and feels almost identical but runs on your own servers. Full data ownership, no per-user cost at scale, and no message history limits. Particularly popular with teams in regulated industries (healthcare, finance, government) where data residency matters.
Best for
Teams with strict data privacy requirements or enterprises where self-hosting is mandatory. Also great for cutting costs at scale.
Free self-hosted · Cloud from $10/user/month
Visit Mattermost
Twist
Async-first team communication — built for focus
Free plan
Twist is built around the idea that constant real-time communication is the problem — not the solution. Organized around threads rather than channels, it encourages async communication, reduces notification anxiety, and gives teams focused, distraction-free work time. Built by the team behind Todoist.
Best for
Remote and distributed teams who are burned out by Slack's real-time pressure and want to shift to a calmer, async communication culture.
Free plan available · Paid from $5/user/month
Visit Twist
Zulip
Thread-first chat — search everything, lose nothing
Free cloud Open source
Zulip's unique thread model means every conversation has a topic — making it impossible to lose important messages. Reddit's analysis of switching discussions consistently identifies Zulip as the top choice for teams that prioritize searchability and structured conversations over real-time chat noise.
Best for
Developer teams and organizations that rely heavily on searchable conversation history and want a thread-first alternative to Slack's chaotic channel model.
Free cloud · Self-hosted free · Business from $6/user/month
Visit Zulip
