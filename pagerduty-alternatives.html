The real reasons
Why engineering teams leave PagerDuty
Based on analysis of G2 reviews, Hacker News discussions, and Reddit engineering communities — these are the most consistent switching triggers.
Reason #1
Pricing escalates fast
PagerDuty starts at $21/user/month and scales steeply. For growing engineering teams with on-call rotations, costs become significant quickly — especially with add-ons for AIOps and analytics.
Reason #2
Complex interface & steep learning curve
Extensive customization options result in a steep learning curve. New engineers joining on-call rotations struggle with the UI — adding stress to already high-pressure incident situations.
Reason #3
Weak collaborative resolution
PagerDuty excels at alerting but is weaker at collaborative incident resolution and post-mortems. Teams end up using Slack alongside PagerDuty anyway — creating tool fragmentation.
Reason #4
Built for engineers only
Non-engineering stakeholders — product managers, customer success, executives — struggle to use PagerDuty for incident visibility. Teams need a separate communication layer.
Reason #5
Migration is painful
Teams delay switching for months because migrating escalation policies, on-call schedules, and integrations is a significant engineering effort — creating vendor lock-in through pain.
Established alternatives
6 proven tools engineering teams switch to

incident.io
Slack-native incident management — declare, manage, resolve, all in Slack
Fastest growingFree plan
incident.io is the most talked-about PagerDuty alternative in 2026 engineering communities. Entirely Slack-native — declare incidents, manage timelines, assign roles, and run post-mortems without leaving Slack. Significantly lower learning curve than PagerDuty, with built-in on-call scheduling included. Teams report going live in hours, not weeks.
Best for
Engineering teams already living in Slack who want incident management without adding another tool their team needs to learn.
Free plan · Pro from $18/user/month
Visit incident.io
Better Stack
Monitoring + on-call + status pages — all in one, significantly cheaper
Free plan
Better Stack combines uptime monitoring, on-call management, and status pages in one platform at a fraction of PagerDuty's cost. Clean interface, fast setup, and generous free tier make it the top choice for startups and growing teams. Escalation policies, on-call schedules, and alert routing all included without add-on fees.
Best for
Startups and growing teams who need monitoring + on-call + status pages without PagerDuty's per-seat pricing and add-on costs.
Free plan · Paid from $24/month
Visit Better Stack
Rootly
Incident management built for modern SRE and DevOps teams
Paid
Rootly is purpose-built for structured incident response workflows — ideal for larger teams with formal SRE practices. Deep Slack and Jira integration, automated runbooks, and rich post-mortem tooling. Consistently recommended for teams that need PagerDuty's depth but with a significantly better user experience.
Best for
Medium to large engineering teams with formal SRE practices who need structured incident workflows, automated runbooks, and detailed post-mortems.
From $19/user/month
Visit Rootly
Grafana Cloud IRM
Native on-call management for Grafana-heavy teams
Free tier
If your team already uses Grafana for monitoring and observability, Grafana Cloud IRM (formerly OnCall) is the most natural PagerDuty replacement. Deeply integrated with Grafana alerts — no separate alerting setup, no context switching. On-call schedules, escalation policies, and mobile app included.
Best for
Teams using Grafana as their primary observability platform who want on-call management native to their existing monitoring stack.
Free tier available · Paid from $299/month
Visit Grafana IRM
Opsgenie (by Atlassian)
PagerDuty's closest feature parity — better Jira integration
Free plan
Opsgenie offers the closest feature parity to PagerDuty at a lower price point, with significantly better Jira integration (both are Atlassian products). For teams already in the Atlassian ecosystem — Jira, Confluence, Bitbucket — Opsgenie is the natural migration path with familiar UI patterns and shared user management.
Best for
Teams in the Atlassian ecosystem who want PagerDuty-equivalent on-call management with native Jira integration at lower cost.
Free up to 5 users · Paid from $9/user/month
Visit Opsgenie
FireHydrant
Incident management + runbooks + retrospectives in one workflow
Free plan
FireHydrant focuses on the full incident lifecycle — not just alerting. From detection through runbooks, communication, and retrospectives, it handles the collaborative resolution process that PagerDuty is weak on. Particularly strong post-mortem tooling that helps teams actually learn from incidents and reduce repeat issues.
Best for
Teams who find PagerDuty good at alerting but weak at collaborative resolution — FireHydrant covers the full incident lifecycle end-to-end.
Free plan · Pro from $18/user/month
Visit FireHydrant
SolarWinds Incident Response
Formerly Squadcast — reliability-focused incident management with strong SRE workflows
Free plan
SolarWinds Incident Response (formerly Squadcast) combines alerting, on-call scheduling, and reliability workflows like SLO tracking and retrospectives in one platform. Strong choice for teams that found PagerDuty's reliability tooling shallow — it bundles incident response with proactive reliability management, often at a lower price point than PagerDuty's equivalent tiers.
Best for
Teams wanting incident response combined with SLO tracking and reliability workflows — without paying for PagerDuty's separate AIOps add-ons.
