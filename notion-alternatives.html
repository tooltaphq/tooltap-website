The real reasons
Why people leave Notion
Based on analysis of thousands of user reviews and community discussions — these are the most common switching triggers.
Reason #1
Too complex to set up
Notion gives you a blank canvas — not a system. Users spend hours building databases before they can actually start working.
Reason #2
Slow performance
Pages load slowly with large databases. On mobile it's even worse — a consistent complaint across thousands of reviews.
Reason #3
Constant maintenance
Once built, Notion requires ongoing attention. Pages pile up, databases go stale — it becomes a graveyard of good intentions.
Reason #4
Pricing for teams
The free plan is generous for solo users but gets expensive quickly for growing teams.
Reason #5
No email or calendar
Notion doesn't connect to your inbox or calendar meaningfully — forcing users to juggle multiple tools.
The best alternatives
6 tools people switch to from Notion
Ranked by how frequently they appear in switching discussions — not by paid placement.
ClickUp
All-in-one productivity — tasks, docs, goals, dashboards
Most switched to Free plan
ClickUp is the most common destination for Notion users switching away. Where Notion gives you a blank canvas, ClickUp gives you structure out of the box — tasks, projects, docs, and goals all in one place without hours of setup.
Best for
Teams tired of building Notion from scratch. If you need project management + docs without setup overhead — ClickUp is your answer.
Free plan available · Paid from $7/user/month
Visit ClickUp
Obsidian
Local-first notes with powerful linking and 900+ plugins
Free for personal
Obsidian stores everything locally as plain Markdown files — no internet required, no subscription, complete data ownership. With bidirectional linking and a thriving plugin ecosystem, it's the choice for people who want full control.
Best for
Solo users, writers, and developers who want a personal knowledge base without cloud dependency or recurring costs.
Free for personal · Sync from $4/month
Visit Obsidian
Coda
Documents that work like apps — with logic and automation
Free plan
Coda combines documents with tables and logic — your doc can become a spreadsheet, app, and automation hub in one. More opinionated than Notion, with built-in automation that removes the manual maintenance Notion requires.
Best for
Teams that want Notion-style docs but with more built-in logic, automation, and interactivity without building everything from scratch.
Free plan available · Paid from $10/user/month
Visit Coda
Slite
AI-powered team knowledge base — ask questions, get answers
AI-native Paid
Slite is built around its AI "Ask" feature — it answers natural-language questions using your team's docs as the source. Purpose-built for internal documentation, far less setup than Notion, and the team actually uses it.
Best for
Teams using Notion primarily as an internal wiki or knowledge base who want AI-powered search across all their docs.
From $8/user/month
Visit Slite
Craft
Beautiful, fast docs — built for Apple users
Free plan
Craft is what Notion would look like if Apple designed it. Blazing fast, beautiful interface, native on Mac and iOS. If Notion's slow performance and clunky mobile experience drove you away — Craft solves both instantly.
Best for
Apple users who want fast, beautiful note-taking and docs without Notion's database-heavy complexity.
Free plan available · Paid from $5/month
Visit Craft
Linear
Purpose-built project management for software teams
Free plan Dev favourite
If you're using Notion primarily for engineering project management — Linear is in a different league. Opinionated, fast, purpose-built for software teams. No blank canvas, no setup — it works the way developers actually think.
Best for
Software and product teams using Notion for issue tracking and sprint planning — Linear is purpose-built for exactly this.
Free for small teams · Paid from $8/user/month
Visit Linear
