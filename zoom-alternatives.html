The real reasons
Why people leave Zoom
Based on analysis of 1,370+ verified Capterra reviews — these are the most cited switching triggers with real sentiment data.
Reason #1
Crashes & freezing
Software crashes, freezing screens, and inconsistent updates causing disruptions mid-meeting — often at the worst possible moment.
59% negative reviews cite bugs & crashes
Reason #2
Audio & mute problems
Echo, unclear audio controls, sound delays, and difficulty managing participant microphones in larger group calls.
57% negative reviews cite audio issues
Reason #3
Connection sensitivity
Zoom is data-intensive — lag, freezing, and dropped calls on anything less than a strong connection. Problematic for teams with remote members in areas with slower internet.
67% negative reviews cite connection issues
Reason #4
Charges for features others give free
Recording, longer meetings, webinars, and advanced features are paywalled — while competitors like Google Meet offer them for free or bundled in existing subscriptions.
Reason #5
Chat history lost after meetings
Important links, notes, and information shared in meeting chat disappear once the call ends — a consistent pain point for teams that rely on meeting chat for real-time collaboration.
Established alternatives
6 proven tools people switch to
Ranked by how frequently they appear in Zoom switching discussions — not by paid placement.
Google Meet
Free, simple, no download required — works in any browser
Most switched to Free
Google Meet is the most common Zoom replacement — particularly for teams already using Google Workspace. No app download required, works directly in the browser, supports up to 100 participants on the free plan with no time limit. Cleaner interface, more stable on weaker connections, and the free tier is significantly more generous than Zoom's.
Best for
Teams on Google Workspace who want a free, stable, browser-based alternative with no download friction for external guests.
Free · Google Workspace from $6/user/month
Visit Google Meet
Microsoft Teams
Enterprise video + chat bundled with Microsoft 365
Free plan
For organizations already using Microsoft 365, Teams is effectively free and deeply integrated with Outlook, OneDrive, and Office apps. Supports meetings with up to 10,000 participants, includes breakout rooms, live captions, transcription, and recording — features Zoom charges extra for.
Best for
Companies already on Microsoft 365 who want to consolidate tools and eliminate a separate Zoom subscription entirely.
Free plan · Microsoft 365 from $6/user/month
Visit Teams
Whereby
Permanent room URL — guests click and join, no download ever
Free plan
Whereby's killer feature is zero friction for guests — you get a permanent room URL, guests click it, and they're in. No app install, no account creation, no "downloading Zoom client" message. For client-facing calls where first impressions matter, this simplicity is unbeatable.
Best for
Consultants, coaches, and client-facing teams who want zero-friction video calls where guests never have to download anything.
Free plan (1 room) · Pro from $6.99/month
Visit Whereby
Jitsi Meet
Open-source, self-hosted, completely free — no account needed
100% Free Open source
Jitsi is the tool privacy communities consistently point to as the gold standard for self-hosted video. WebRTC-based, Apache 2.0 licensed, completely free — self-host on your own infrastructure or use the free meet.jit.si instance. When you self-host, all metadata, logs, and recordings stay on your servers.
Best for
Privacy-focused teams, regulated industries, and developers who want complete data sovereignty and zero per-user cost.
Completely free · Self-hosted or cloud
Visit Jitsi
Webex
Enterprise-grade video with AI features — by Cisco
Free plan AI-powered
Webex is Cisco's enterprise video platform — consistently cited as a Zoom alternative in Capterra reviews by enterprise users. Strong AI features including real-time translation, noise cancellation, and meeting summaries. More stable than Zoom on enterprise networks, better customer support, and a free plan that's comparable to Zoom's.
Best for
Enterprise teams who need carrier-grade reliability, compliance features, and AI-powered meeting intelligence beyond what Zoom offers.
Free plan · Paid from $14.50/user/month
Visit Webex
Discord
Persistent voice channels — join and leave without scheduling
Free
Discord offers persistent voice channels — your team joins and leaves at will, no scheduling required. Unlike Zoom's scheduled meeting model, Discord feels like a virtual office where people are just "in the room." Increasingly used by startups and dev teams for spontaneous collaboration and daily standups.
Best for
Startups and dev teams who want a virtual office feel with always-on voice channels — replacing both Zoom meetings and Slack simultaneously.
Free · Nitro from $9.99/month
Visit Discord
