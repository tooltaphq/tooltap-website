The real reasons
Why people leave Trello
Based on analysis of thousands of verified reviews — these are the most consistent switching triggers in 2026.
Reason #1
10-board free plan limit
The most common trigger — teams hit 10 boards and face either paying or switching. Growing teams find this ceiling arrives faster than expected.
Reason #2
No Gantt or timeline view
Trello is Kanban-only. No native Gantt charts, no dependency views, no timeline. Teams with deadline-driven projects have to use Power-Ups or switch entirely.
Reason #3
No built-in time tracking
Time tracking requires third-party Power-Ups — adding cost and complexity. Teams that bill by the hour or need resource management find Trello's gaps painful.
Reason #4
Boards become a mess
A 28-person agency managing 47 Trello boards is a documented real-world case. Without structure, Trello scales into chaos — old cards pile up with no clear ownership or status.
Reason #5
Pricing scales aggressively
55% of negative Capterra reviews cite pricing — key features locked behind higher tiers with support response times stretching to weeks for billing disputes.
Established alternatives
6 proven tools people switch to
Ranked by switching frequency — not paid placement.
ClickUp
Everything Trello lacks — Gantt, time tracking, unlimited boards, docs
Most switched toFree plan
ClickUp is the most complete Trello replacement in 2026 — Kanban boards, Gantt charts, time tracking, subtasks, and a docs layer all in one platform. The free plan is genuinely usable with unlimited tasks, unlimited members, and no board cap. Direct Trello board import available. Most non-technical teams find their footing within a week.
Best for
Teams replacing Trello and wanting everything in one tool — unlimited boards, Gantt views, and time tracking without paying for multiple tools.
Free plan · Unlimited $7/user/month
Visit ClickUp
Notion
Kanban boards + docs + databases — all in one workspace
Free plan
For teams using Trello primarily as a content or project board, Notion offers Kanban views alongside docs, wikis, and databases. Everything lives in one place — no more context switching between Trello and Google Docs. Note: Notion has its own switching frustrations (see our Notion alternatives page) so evaluate carefully.
Best for
Content teams, solo creators, and small teams who want Kanban boards alongside documentation and databases in one workspace.
Free plan · Plus $8/user/month
Visit Notion
monday.com
Visual workflows — the upgrade path most agencies choose
Free plan
monday.com is the most consistent recommendation for teams where the primary Trello pain is lack of views and reporting. A project manager checking deadlines sees a Gantt. A team member reviewing tasks sees a filtered board. For teams forcing Trello to do more than it was designed for — monday.com fills that gap most directly.
Best for
Agencies and teams managing multiple client projects who need timeline views, reporting, and automation that Trello's Power-Up model can't provide.
Free plan · Basic $9/user/month
Visit monday.com
Asana
Multiple views, task dependencies, timeline — Trello's natural upgrade
Free plan
Asana is the most natural upgrade from Trello — familiar card-based thinking, but with timeline views, task dependencies, and reporting that Trello lacks. Free plan is generous, the interface is clean, and most Trello users are productive in Asana within days. Direct Trello import makes migration painless.
Best for
Teams who love Trello's visual approach but need dependency management, timeline views, and better reporting without the complexity of Jira.
Free plan · Premium $10.99/user/month
Visit Asana
Planka
Open-source Trello — self-hosted, free, identical interface
FreeOpen source
Planka is the open-source Trello clone — almost identical interface but self-hostable on your own server. If you love Trello's simplicity but are leaving due to pricing or the 10-board limit — Planka gives you unlimited boards with zero subscription cost. Ideal for teams with a developer who can handle a simple server setup.
Best for
Teams who genuinely love Trello's simplicity and are only leaving due to the 10-board free limit or privacy requirements.
Completely free · Self-hosted
Visit Planka
Linear
For dev teams outgrowing Trello's simplicity
Free plan
When engineering teams outgrow Trello, Linear is where they go — not Jira. Faster, cleaner, purpose-built for software development with cycles, roadmaps, and GitHub integration. If your Trello boards are tracking engineering work and they've become unwieldy, Linear provides structure without Jira's complexity.
Best for
Engineering teams using Trello for software development who need sprint management and GitHub integration without switching to Jira.
Free plan · Pro $8/user/month
Visit Linear
